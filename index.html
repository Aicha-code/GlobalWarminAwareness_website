Earth is warming up, and humans are at least partially to blame. The causes, effects, and complexities of global warming are important to understand so that we can fight for the health of our planet.

Global warming is the long-term heating of the planet’s overall temperature observed since the pre-industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) its pace has significantly increased in the last hundred years due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning. As the human population has increased, so has the volume of fossil fuels burned. Fossil fuels include coal, oil, and natural gas, and burning them causes what is known as the “greenhouse effect” in Earth’s atmosphere. This term is not interchangeable with the term "climate change."

Global warming is the long-term warming of the planet’s overall temperature. Though this warming trend has been going on for a long time, its pace has significantly increased in the last hundred years due to the burning of fossil fuels.
